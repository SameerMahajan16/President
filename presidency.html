Presidency

Jimmy Carter served as the 39th president of the United States from 1977 to 1981. His term focused on human rights, peace efforts, and environmental protection.

He achieved peace in the Middle East with the Camp David Accords and created the Department of Energy and Education.

Carter also faced challenges like the Iran Hostage Crisis and a struggling economy with high inflation and unemployment.

Source: Carter Center
